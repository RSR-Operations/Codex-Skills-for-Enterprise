# Automation Opportunity Patterns

## Automation Types

- **Codex Skill**: repeatable judgment or synthesis workflow.
- **Script**: deterministic transformation, extraction, validation, or formatting task.
- **Integration**: workflow that depends on system-to-system movement.
- **Template**: standardized artifact that reduces drafting and review time.
- **Dashboard**: recurring visibility problem with measurable data.
- **Process redesign**: workflow is unclear, redundant, or poorly owned before automation.

## Prioritization Matrix

| Category | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| Quick win | High value, low complexity, low risk |
| Strategic bet | High value, higher complexity, requires staged rollout |
| Defer | Useful, but blocked by data, ownership, or process maturity |
| Avoid | Low value, high risk, or inappropriate for automation |

## Intake Questions

- What triggers the workflow?
- How often does it happen?
- Which artifacts are inputs and outputs?
- Where does quality fail today?
- What decisions require human judgment?
- What would make the automation trustworthy?
